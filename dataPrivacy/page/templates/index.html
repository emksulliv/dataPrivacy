Data Privacy

What does this all mean?

Data Collection: So how does this work?

Media platforms allow for users to create media accounts where they can provide behavioral, preference, and demographic data about themselves. This data can be collected from the things you post, like, accept or search about through your devices. Big data companies and scientists then collect this data and build personas about you that can determine your age and gender, what you like and much more.

Essentially, big data companies are creating computer-based personalities based on information collected from your devices, that end up knowing more about you than your family and friends do.


Where does my data go?

To the highest bidder! No seriously. The collection and distribution of user data is sold by social media services to buyers who may be interested in using it.

Ways we know user data is being used:

  • Advertising

    • ever talk about a nice watch with your coworkers then 10 minutes later you get 10 ads on facebook about that same brand watch? This is tech called "targeted ads".

    anything you search or say will be used to try to place you into a consumer category

  • Scientific studies

    • Labs and organizations will use data from social media sites to use on statistical experiments and findings

  • Internal Company Research

    • How often are you clicking on your profile page? Or others? Social media companies will use their own user's data to better their own services

  • Profit (to data brokers or other companies)

    • Here's the profit bit we mentioned earlier, data brokers collect your online data -- everything from your name and address to your income and general internect activity -- and sell it to other brokers or businesses

  • be stored indefinitely

    • ever stored you credit card number on a shopping site you use to ease the checkout process int eh future? Most companies do not disclose when (if ever) they will get rid of that data so it is stored indefinitely in their servers.
